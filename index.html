Since starting my studies and experiencing product design in industry, I have become increasingly interested in medical design, and particularly in design for women. A long term goal is to design for parity within the medical industry, ensuring products are equally ethical, usable, comfortable and safe for both men and women. My research revolves around the donation of menstrual blood for cell therapy, and working to make the future donation of this blood easy, safe, painless, and cost effective. Looking at human factors as well as the practical factors of donating this blood sample ensuring cell survival, I am learning how women can be supported throughout this process and the various influences that affect whether or not someone will donate.

    Farres Yasser
    BSc (Honours) Architectural systems Engineering, MSc Sustainable Building Engineering

    Farres is a PhD candidate working under Dr.Anton Ianakiev's supervision. His Ph.D. focuses on developing insulation panels using recycled materials. He has a record of lecturing in various fields that touch directly on sustainable development, from design studios to technical drawings, 3d modelling software's to urban design elective courses where his main focus helped him develop a curriculum for students to slowly but surely embrace the sustainable development discourse in both high tech civic buildings and in makeshift, juxta-positioned informal settlements which make up the majority of the globe's settlements.

    He spent most of his career in Cairo, Egypt working with informal settlement residents to design, develop, simulate, construct, test and evaluate novel interventions designed to enhance the performance of buildings in disenfranchised settlements. His work is in the field of using both reused and recycled materials which are both biodegradable and non biodegradable for thermal insulation, solar heating techniques, daylight and sunlight control as well as energy use mitigation. He has delivered a number of conference presentations, peer reviewed journal publications and funded projects to develop design strategies that would help improve the quality of life through thermal comfort in informal settlements.

    Farres spends his leisure time focusing on his professional poetry writing, and in obtaining general knowledge in the fields of economics, nutrition, psychology, technology and, of course, engineering.

    He hosts his own podcast (Purecast.org) which is featured on several media platforms which promotes the open discussion of everyday life topics and trends leading to deeper and better understanding of our role in it and the meaning behind it.
